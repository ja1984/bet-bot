### What Went Wrong

- 8 consecutive wins, 17 straight home wins vs Getafe — the data was overwhelming
- But streaks end, and this Madrid side is missing Mbappé AND Bellingham
- Rated Real Madrid Win at 9/10 — far too high given their weakened squad and recent Osasuna loss
- **Lesson:** H2H records create false confidence when the current squad is significantly weakened

**2. Underestimated Bordalás's Getafe**
- Correctly identified Getafe as defensive and low-scoring, but didn't account for their counter-attacking threat
- Satriano's quality finish shows Getafe DO have individual quality up front
- Bordalás got his tactics perfect — compact, disciplined, lethal on the counter
- **Lesson:** "Can't score" ≠ "won't score." Even low-scoring teams can produce a moment of magic

**3. Every combo depended on a Madrid win**
- All 4 combos required Real Madrid to win — zero diversification
- The warning about single-match risk was noted but not acted upon
- Should have included a "Madrid fails" hedge: e.g. Under 2.5 Goals as a standalone, or Draw/Getafe double chance + Under 2.5
- **Lesson:** With only one game, at least one combo MUST survive if the favourite loses

**4. Vinícius hype**
- Correctly noted Bordalás would double-mark him, but still included him in 2 combos
- Vinícius had an early 1-on-1 chance but Soria saved — that was his best moment
- Getafe's deep block neutralized him effectively
- **Lesson:** Form streaks are less relevant against bus-parking teams

### What Went Right

### Key Takeaway
> The defensive analysis was excellent. The attacking assumption (Madrid will score and win) was lazy and over-relied on historical dominance. **Next time with a single match: always hedge with at least one combo that survives a favourite's loss.**
